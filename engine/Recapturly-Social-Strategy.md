## 1. Audience & voice

**Who:** Shopify store owners and ecommerce operators doing ~$20k–$500k+/month, leaving 15–30% of revenue in uncollected carts and lapsed customers. Busy, agency-skeptical, allergic to fluff. Secondary: marketing managers / DTC operators who influence the decision.

**Voice:** Professional and trusted with a direct, no-fluff edge — the website voice ("Stop watching sales walk out the door"). Sharp operator who respects the reader's time, leads with numbers, never overpromises.

**Angles we own:** revenue recovery (not "email marketing") · "open rates are vanity, revenue recovered is the metric" · Shopify + Klaviyo only, done obsessively well · "AI spots the opportunity, a human writes the email" · live in 48 hours.

---

## 2. The value-vs-promotion model — 70 / 20 / 10

The old flat 80/20 rule underperforms in 2026; the stronger framework is a three-way split, and it maps cleanly to a 7-post week per platform:

- **70% pure value (5 of 7 posts):** education, tactics, hot takes, POV, engagement. No ask. This is what earns reach, saves, and trust.
- **20% soft proof (1 of 7):** anonymized results, mini case studies, before/after flow rebuilds. Sells without selling.
- **10% direct ask (1 of 7):** book the free audit. One clean CTA per platform per week — no more.

Why this matters: every platform's 2026 algorithm now **penalizes overtly promotional content** and rewards saves, shares, and replies. If we lead with value, the occasional ask actually converts. If we lead with asks, reach collapses.

## 3. Human-sounding rules (non-negotiable)

LinkedIn alone gives AI-detected content **47% less reach**, and readers clock the AI tells instantly. Every post has to pass as written by a sharp human operator. The three structural habits below are the biggest giveaways, bigger than any single word.

**The three patterns that scream AI (kill these first):**
1. **Em-dashes.** The number-one fingerprint. Almost no real person texts in em-dashes. Use a period, a comma, parentheses, or rewrite. Hard cap: one em-dash per post, ideally zero.
2. **The "it's not X, it's Y" negation flip** (antithesis). "It's not a traffic problem, it's a follow-up problem." "Not just a tool, a transformation." AI defaults to this because it sounds profound without making an argument. Cap: once per *week* across all posts, never as a hook.
3. **Forced rule-of-three triplets.** "Strategy, copy, and design." "Speed, efficiency, and scale." AI pads lists to three. Use two, or four, or just one. Break the rhythm.

**Also banned (vocabulary tells):**
- Connector crutches: "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover," "In today's fast-paced world."
- Fake-suspense lines: "But here's the kicker," "Here's the thing," "Plot twist."
- LLM slop words: "delve," "realm," "tapestry," "landscape," "unlock," "leverage," "elevate," "supercharge," "seamless," "robust," "game-changer," "navigating the world of."
- Hollow empathy openers: "Picture this," "As a store owner, you know," "We've all been there."
- The "Header: explanation" list rhythm.

**Required (what humans actually do):**
- Contractions everywhere. Fragments are fine. Start a sentence with "And" or "But."
- Open with a concrete hook: a number, a blunt claim, or a specific scene. Read line one out loud. If the reaction is "so what?", rewrite it.
- Specific, slightly messy detail over clean abstraction. "The win-back got toggled off after a theme update and nobody noticed" beats "flows can break."
- Vary sentence length so it has a pulse. One idea per post.

## 4. Platform playbooks

### LinkedIn
- **What wins:** concrete expertise — real data, real examples, actionable takeaways — gets **3.7x the reach** of generic content. Document/carousel posts hit the **highest engagement of any format (~6.6%)**.
- **Hook:** first 2–3 lines decide the "see more" click. Front-load the tension.
- **Length:** 150–400 words. White space between lines.
- **Hashtags:** 3 max. (More than 5 cuts reach ~68%.)
- **Saves are the #1 signal in 2026** — write reference-worthy posts people bookmark.
- **Series beat one-offs:** thematically linked posts in sequence earn **62% more cumulative engagement**. We'll run recurring threads (e.g. "the 4 flows," one per week).
- **Cadence/timing:** 7/wk; lean into Tue–Thu 8–10am ET.

### Instagram (images & carousels only — no video)
- **Reality check:** Reels are the discovery engine and we're not using them, so **carousels are our growth engine** — they pull **10.2% engagement vs 7% for single images.** We go carousel-heavy.
- **Carousels:** 6–10 slides, hook in the first 3 slides, end on a CTA/recap slide (+20–30% engagement). Make the last slide saveable.
- **Caption hook:** first 10–12 words, before the "more" cutoff. Then 150–300 words for educational posts.
- **Caption SEO:** keyword-rich captions are now the main discovery path — write naturally but include "Shopify," "Klaviyo," "abandoned cart," etc.
- **Goal metric:** saves, shares, DM sends — not likes.
- **Hashtags:** 8–12, mix broad + niche.
- **Mix:** ~5 carousels + 2 single images per week.

### Facebook (images & carousels only)
- **Reality check:** organic reach leans hard on video here, which we're skipping — so Facebook is our lowest-reach platform and we treat it as repurpose + community, not the growth driver. Teaching/story carousels and photos that earn **saves and DM shares** are what still travels.
- **Links:** put the audit link in the **first comment**, never the post body — link-in-body posts get the lowest reach.
- **80/20 minimum** on promo; respond to comments fast (signals genuine interaction).
- **Cadence/timing:** 7/wk; Tue–Wed, early morning ET perform best.

## 4b. Visual system & feed aesthetics

All graphics come from one locked engine using the Brand Kit: Deep Navy backgrounds, Electric Teal as the hero/CTA color, Soft Gold sparingly (never on the wordmark), Plus Jakarta Sans display, Inter body, Lora italic for quotes.

**Five tile treatments to rotate:** dark stat card · light stat card · quote card (Lora) · email mockup (product proof) · tagline/brand tile. Carousels use the cover/body/CTA set.

**Grid rules (Instagram-focused, since it's the only grid view):**
- Never place two of the same treatment side by side.
- Alternate dark navy and light off-white tiles in a loose checkerboard so the feed has rhythm, not a wall of navy.
- Stat cards and quotes carry the message; the email mockup and tagline tiles are palate cleansers every 3–4 posts.
- LinkedIn and Facebook are single-feed, not grids, so we just vary post-to-post there and weight grid effort toward Instagram.

**Roadmap (per Brand Kit):** as real results arrive, fold in actual Klaviyo recovered-revenue screenshots and a founder/behind-the-scenes photo. Those are the "real image" posts and they outperform templates because they're proof. Email mockups stand in until then.

## 5. Content pillars (mapped to the 70/20/10 model)

**Value (70%):**
1. **Revenue-recovery education** — how the flows work, the stats, what "good" looks like.
2. **Klaviyo/email tactics** — actionable, save-worthy tips (timing, subject lines, sequence structure).
3. **Hot takes / POV** — reasoned contrarian opinions that invite replies ("discounting in email #1 is a mistake").
4. **Engagement** — questions, polls, "which flow are you missing?"

**Soft proof (20%):**
5. **Results & rebuilds** — anonymized "recovered $X," before/after flow audits.

**Direct ask (10%):**
6. **Free audit CTA** — one clean invite per platform per week.

## 8. What we measure

Not likes. The signals the 2026 algorithms reward and that map to the business:
- **Saves + shares + DM sends** (the reach driver across all three)
- **Comments + DMs** (conversation depth is weighted heavily)
- **Profile visits → link clicks → free-audit bookings** (the only revenue metric that matters)

Monthly: which pillars and hooks drove saves and bookings → double down, cut the rest.
